- Fire extinguisher gauge shows red (undercharged)

### The Guide: AlarmBeepGuide

**Positioning:**
- Trusted safety mentor with plain-English explanations
- No judgment, no jargon, no assumption of prior knowledge

- Clear guidance on when DIY is safe vs. when to call professionals

**Authority Signals:**
- References NFPA standards without technical overwhelm
- Cites manufacturer guidelines in accessible language
- Explains "why" behind recommendations, not just "what"
- Acknowledges limitations and directs to professionals when appropriate

1. **Match the Pattern**: Identify your extinguisher's specific issue (gauge reading, age, physical condition)
2. **Understand What It Means**: Learn whether it's urgent, routine, or informational
3. **Fix It Safely**: Follow tested steps with clear guidance on DIY vs. professional help

### Stakes

**Failure (What's at Risk):**
- Relying on expired or undercharged extinguisher during fire emergency
- Fire extinguisher fails when seconds count
- Property damage that could have been prevented
- Insurance complications from improperly maintained safety equipment
- Failed rental/commercial property inspections

**Success (Transformation):**
- Confidence that fire extinguisher will work when needed
- Peace of mind from proper maintenance habits

- Family safety protected through informed equipment management

  - ADA accessibility considerations
  - Why height matters (reach + visibility)
