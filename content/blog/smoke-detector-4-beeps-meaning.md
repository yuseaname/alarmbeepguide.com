# Smoke Detector 4 Beeps: What the 4-Beep Pattern Means

If your smoke detector is sounding 4 beeps — either as a continuous alarm pattern or as a periodic chirp sequence — the meaning depends on the alarm type and brand. This guide covers every common 4-beep scenario for US smoke alarms and combination detectors.

**Bottom line upfront:** On most combination smoke/CO alarms, 4 beeps followed by a pause is the standard carbon monoxide alarm pattern. If you hear 4 repeating beeps on a combination detector, treat it as a CO emergency and evacuate immediately.

## Quick Reference: What 4 Beeps Usually Mean

| Detector Type | 4-Beep Pattern | Meaning |
|--------------|---------------|---------|
| Smoke-only detector | 4 beeps repeating continuously | Fire alarm — evacuate now |
| Combination smoke/CO | 4 beeps, pause, 4 beeps (repeating) | CO alarm — evacuate, call 911 |
| Any detector | 4 chirps, then silence, repeating every 30–60 seconds | Battery, fault, or end-of-life maintenance alert |
| Kidde (interconnected) | 4 beeps then silence, every few minutes | Possible interconnect fault code |

---

## If You're Hearing 4 Beeps Right Now

**If the beeping is loud, continuous, and urgent:** This is potentially a fire or CO alarm. Follow this immediately:

1. **Do not assume it's a false alarm.** Treat all loud, continuous alarm tones as real emergencies until proven otherwise.
2. **For CO alarm:** Get everyone (including pets) out of the building immediately. Do not stop to collect belongings. Call 911 from outside.
3. **For fire alarm:** Follow your home's fire escape plan. Do not open doors that are warm to the touch. Call 911 from outside.

**If the 4-beep sound is a soft, short chirp repeating every 30–60 seconds:** This is a maintenance alert, not an emergency. Read the section below on periodic chirping.

## 4 Beeps on a Combination Smoke/CO Alarm

Most modern US households use combination detectors that detect both smoke and carbon monoxide. On the vast majority of combination alarms — including Kidde and First Alert models — the alarm pattern indicates:

- **3 beeps (continuous):** Smoke detected
- **4 beeps (continuous, repeating):** Carbon monoxide detected

This is the most important thing to know about 4-beep patterns. If you have a combination detector and it's giving 4 continuous beeps, you need to evacuate for carbon monoxide, not just fire.

### Carbon monoxide is odorless and colorless

Unlike smoke, you cannot smell or see carbon monoxide. The alarm may be the only warning you get. Never silence a CO alarm and go back to sleep. The consequences of ignoring a real CO event include serious injury or death.

### Common sources of CO triggering a 4-beep alarm

- Gas furnace or boiler malfunction
- Vehicle running in an attached garage
- Gas fireplace, water heater, or dryer venting improperly
- Generator running indoors or near an open window
- Charcoal grills used indoors

## 4 Beeps on a Smoke-Only Detector

If your detector is a smoke-only model (no CO sensing), 4 continuous beeps mean the same as any number of continuous beeps: fire alarm. Evacuate.

However, smoke-only detectors rarely use 4 beeps specifically as a diagnostic pattern — they typically produce continuous beeping during an alarm. If you're hearing exactly 4 beeps followed by silence, repeating periodically, you may be looking at:

- A specific fault code (check your detector's manual)
- A low battery alert that happens to chirp 4 times (some models do this)
- An interconnected detector signaling a fault elsewhere in the system

## 4 Chirps as a Maintenance Alert

Some smoke alarm models use 4 chirps as a specific maintenance code rather than the common 1 chirp every 30–60 seconds. If you're hearing:

- 4 soft chirps
- Then silence for a minute or more
- Then 4 chirps again

Check these possible causes in this order:

**1. Low battery**
This remains the most common cause of any chirp pattern. Replace the battery with a fresh 9V alkaline or AA/AAA cells depending on your model. After replacement, hold the test button for 5–10 seconds to reset the unit.

**2. End of life**
If your unit is more than 7–10 years old and chirping persists after battery replacement, the detector has reached end of life and needs replacement. Check the manufacture date on the back or inside the battery compartment.

**3. Fault condition**
Some detectors use a 4-chirp pattern to signal a sensor fault or chamber contamination. Try cleaning the sensing chamber vents with compressed air. If chirping persists, replace the unit.

## Brand-Specific 4-Beep Meanings

### Kidde

On Kidde combination (smoke/CO) alarms: 4 beeps is the standard CO alarm pattern. On Kidde smoke-only alarms: 4 beeps may indicate an interconnect signal (one unit in a linked system signaling the others about a fault at the initiating unit). Check your specific model's manual at kidde.com.

### First Alert

On First Alert combination alarms: 4 beeps followed by pause is the CO alarm signal. On First Alert smoke-only alarms: 4 beeps is less standardized — check the model manual. First Alert's online support page allows you to search by model number for your specific alarm code.

### Nest Protect

Nest Protect uses voice alerts and app notifications rather than a standard beep code. If you're hearing alarm tones from a Nest Protect, check the Nest app immediately — it will identify whether it's a smoke or CO event, and which room.

### X-Sense

X-Sense combination alarms follow similar conventions to Kidde and First Alert — 4 beeps for CO alarm. X-Sense provides downloadable manuals on their website for each model.

## How to Stop 4 Beeps and Address the Cause

**For a CO alarm (4 continuous beeps, urgent):**
1. Evacuate everyone from the building immediately
2. Call 911 from outside
3. Do not re-enter until emergency responders have cleared the building and identified the CO source
4. The CO source must be repaired by a qualified technician before re-occupying

**For a smoke alarm (4 continuous beeps):**
1. Follow your fire escape plan
2. Call 911 from outside

**For periodic 4-chirp maintenance alerts:**
1. Replace the battery (9V alkaline or as specified)
2. Reset the unit by holding the test button 10–15 seconds after replacement
3. Test by pressing the test button — it should emit a full alarm tone
4. If chirping persists: check manufacture date and replace unit if 7+ years old

---

## When to Replace vs Reset

If you're dealing with periodic 4-chirp alerts and you've replaced the battery:

| Manufacture Date | Action |
|-----------------|--------|
| Less than 7 years old | Reset (discharge, new battery, test) + clean chamber |
| 7–10 years old | Replace the unit |
| More than 10 years old | Replace immediately — legally and functionally overdue |

Smoke detector replacement is not optional from a safety standpoint. Detectors older than 10 years have degraded sensors that may not reliably detect smoke. NFPA 72 recommends replacing smoke alarms every 10 years (8 years for combination alarms).

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What does 4 beeps mean on a smoke alarm?**

On a combination smoke/CO alarm (the most common type in US homes), 4 continuous repeating beeps is the carbon monoxide alarm signal. On a smoke-only alarm, 4 continuous beeps is a fire alarm. If the 4 beeps are soft, short, and repeat every minute or so, this is a maintenance alert — most likely low battery or end-of-life warning.

**Why is my smoke detector chirping 4 times then stopping?**

Four soft chirps followed by silence, repeating every 30–60 seconds, typically indicate a maintenance issue: low battery, end-of-life warning, or sensor fault. Start by replacing the battery with a fresh unit. If the pattern continues after a reset, check the manufacture date — detectors older than 7 years should be replaced.

**Is 4 beeps on a combination detector always CO?**

On most combination smoke/CO alarms from major US brands (Kidde, First Alert, X-Sense), yes — 4 continuous beeps followed by a pause is the standard CO pattern. However, specific model behavior can vary. The safest approach is always to treat 4 continuous beeps as a CO alarm and evacuate, then verify once you're safely outside.

**My smoke detector beeps 4 times at night only — why?**

Night-time chirping that doesn't happen during the day is almost always a temperature-related battery issue. Batteries lose voltage as temperatures drop overnight, triggering low-battery alerts. Even if the battery seems fine during the day, the overnight voltage drop falls below the threshold that triggers the alarm. Replace the battery with a fresh alkaline — do not use rechargeable batteries in smoke detectors.
